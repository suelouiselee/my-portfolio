Koreatown Plaza

Home | Back to Projects

The space that I chose to model is the Koreatown Plaza, a shopping mall located in Koreatown, Los Angeles. It first opened in 1988 as the first major indoor shopping center in Koreatown. I have memories of going there every Sunday after church to go grocery shopping with my family and if I got lucky, go to Artbox and grow my food eraser collection. The mall is straight out of the 80s and holds no semblance to malls in South Korea, and today, the stores are mostly empty and receive little foot traffic. This project gave me the opportunity to explore the Koreatown Plaza in the context of a liminal space and the absence of performance and ritual.
